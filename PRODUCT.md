# Product

## Register

product

## Users

General tech audience — developers, enthusiasts, and curious professionals who want to stay on top of AI agent developments without wading through noise. They check in during breaks or at the start of the day, scanning for what matters and drilling into what's interesting. Speed of comprehension is the primary UX goal.

## Product Purpose

A curated link aggregator for AI agent use cases in enterprise and business. Surfaces articles about real-world agentic system implementations — companies writing about how and why they deployed agents for internal documentation, collaboration tools, customer support, and other operational workflows. All content links to external sources (opens in new tab). The MVP displays rich metadata: favicons, titles, descriptions, and OG images from the linked articles. Success looks like: a user opens the site, scans the feed, finds a relevant case study, and clicks through to read it.

## Theme

Dark mode only. The interface is consumed on screens, often by developers and tech professionals working in dark environments.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, informed, fast. Wire-service energy — crisp headlines, dense but scannable layout, zero fluff. The interface should feel like a well-run newsroom desk, not a lifestyle magazine.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing pages**: no hero metrics, gradient CTAs, "trusted by" social proof, or template aesthetics. This is a tool, not a pitch deck.
- **Overly minimal/empty layouts**: bare-bones markdown-rendered pages with no design craft. Efficient does not mean featureless — the design should be purposeful and refined, just not decorative.
- **Social media feeds**: not Twitter/X or Reddit. No infinite scroll engagement loops, algorithmic chaos, or attention-hijacking patterns.

## Design Principles

1. **Information density over decoration** — every pixel earns its place by communicating something. Whitespace is a tool for scanability, not filler.
2. **Speed of comprehension** — a user should grasp the page state in under two seconds. Headlines, hierarchy, and grouping do the heavy lifting.
3. **Respect the reader's time** — no engagement tricks, no dark patterns, no unnecessary friction. Get in, get informed, get out.
4. **Quiet authority** — the interface signals competence through precision and craft, not through loudness or novelty.
5. **Function shapes form** — layout, typography, and color choices follow from what the content needs, not from aesthetic trends.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. 4.5:1 minimum contrast for body text, keyboard navigation support, screen reader compatibility. Reduced motion alternatives for any animations. Color should never be the sole indicator of state.
